Understanding Stars

Properties of Light

Blackbody Spectra

  • Blackbody: a solid or very dense object that is not reflective, but can absorb and emit light. Stars, planets, people are approximate blackbodies.

  • Spectrum: Light spread into its component wavelengths (by a prism).

  • Blackbodies emit light based on their temperature: Hotter objects emit most of their light at shorter wavelengths, cooler objects emit most of their light at longer wavelengths.

Types of Spectra Spectra

  • Continuous: Light from a blackbody shines through a prism (All colors are seen)

  • Absorption: Light from a blackbody shines through a cooler gas, then through a prism (All colors are seen but dark lines can be seen)

  • Emission: Light from hot gas shines through a prism (Only some color can be seen)

Why do we see this?

When light passes through gas, atoms in the gas can only absorb or emit specific wavelengths of light. The wavelengths absorbed or emitted are specific to each element independent of temperature. By looking at the absorption or emission lines, elements can be identified. The continuous spectrum shows temperature and relative size while absorption and emission show composition.


Doppler shift

Blueshift: When a source of a wave moves towards an observer, the waves get squeezed together.

Redshift: When a source of a wave moves away from an observer, the waves get stretched.


The faster an object is moving towards or away from an observer, the greater the Doppler shift will be. Stars do not move fast enough for the Doppler shift to change their colors. Spectrial lines are observed to see change for stars.
